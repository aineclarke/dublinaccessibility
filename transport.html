Dublin Transport Services tend to be quite accomodating of wheelchair users, although there are some limitations,
so we hope to provide you with the best possible information on getting from one place to antother aswell as some other alterntives if needed.

Bus

The main provider of bus services in Dublin, the publicly-owned Dublin Bus, uses low-floor, wheelchair-accessible buses on ALL of its routes.
Additionally, Dublin Bus runs a travel assistance scheme to help those with a mobility impairment to travel around Dublin using either bus, train or tram. An assistant can accompany you the first few times you travel, and give you advice on planning a journey.
The Travel Assistance Scheme is free and is for people aged 18 or over. You can use it Monday to Friday between 08:00hrs and 18:00hrs.

Bus Eireann is Ireland’s main provider of long-distance coach services, many of which operate in the Greater Dublin area.
Although many of its services are fully wheelchair accessible, not all are.
You can find the full list of wheelchair friendly routes fromthis link.

Trains

Trains in Dublin are wheelchair accessible, as are most of the stations. Staff can usually assist with the boarding of trains via the provision of wheelchair ramps too.
However, depending on station staffing levels, help is not always at hand. Irish Rail therefore ask those with a mobility impairment to contact them in advance of travel to ensure that full support with your journey is provided.
You can do so by emailingaccess@irishrail.ie

Luas

Dublin’s tram system, ‘Luas,’ has been designed to be fully accessible and it is the easiest mode of transport in Dublin to use if you have a mobility impairment.

Taxi

Taxis and wheelchair accessible taxis are public hire vehicles.
Their licence and licensing requirements allow them to stand for hire at a rank (taxi ranks at airports, train and bus stations) and they can also be hailed on the street.
The vehicle is equipped with a taximeter to ensure the maximum fare charged is within regulations.

It is also possible to book private owned wheelchair accessible taxis from great Dublin owned company called "dublin Wheelchair Taxis".
You can find there website from the followinglink.

"Planning a Journey"

Before you start your journey, you can useTransport for Ireland’s online journey plannerto plan your journey.
The journey planner will give you information about bus and rail routes all over Ireland.
It will also tell you if a service is wheelchair accessible.
If you can’t travel alone, you may be able to get a free travel pass for your travel companion.
